# INFO-2311 — Networking Technologies 2

**Program:** KPU Computer Information Systems Diploma  
**Topic Area:** Advanced Networking & Security

## What This Course Covered

- Advanced routing protocols: OSPF, EIGRP concepts, BGP fundamentals
- VLAN design and inter-VLAN routing: Layer 3 switches, router-on-a-stick
- WAN technologies: MPLS, SD-WAN concepts, leased lines, broadband
- VPN configuration: IPSec site-to-site, SSL/TLS remote access VPN
- Wireless networking: 802.11 standards (a/b/g/n/ac/ax), WPA2/WPA3, wireless controllers
- Quality of Service (QoS): traffic prioritization for VoIP and critical applications

## Key Skills Developed

- Designing multi-VLAN environments with proper segmentation and inter-VLAN routing
- Configuring and troubleshooting IPSec VPNs between sites
- Selecting appropriate WAN technologies for client connectivity requirements
- Diagnosing wireless connectivity issues at the RF and configuration level

## How This Applies to My Career

This course is the most directly mapped course to my infrastructure work. At **F12.net** as an Infrastructure Specialist, VLAN design and VPN configuration were regular project deliverables. At **ESW IT**, I configure and troubleshoot **SonicWall** firewalls and **WatchGuard** appliances — both of which implement the VPN, routing, and firewall concepts from this course.

The WatchGuard cert I am currently pursuing (PCNSE) builds directly on the advanced networking foundation from INFO-2311, going deeper on threat management and enterprise security policy that this course introduced.
