# INFO-1111 — Computer Hardware & Software

**Program:** KPU Computer Information Systems Diploma  
**Topic Area:** Hardware Fundamentals & OS Installation

## What This Course Covered

- Internal hardware components: CPU, RAM, storage (HDD/SSD), motherboard, PSU, GPU, and expansion cards
- Peripheral identification, setup, and configuration (monitors, printers, input devices)
- Operating system installation procedures — clean installs, partitioning, driver management
- Structured troubleshooting methodology: identify, establish theory, test, establish plan, implement, verify, document
- BIOS/UEFI configuration, boot order, and firmware updates

## Key Skills Developed

- Diagnosing hardware failures and component-level troubleshooting
- Imaging and configuring systems to a defined standard
- Documenting hardware configurations and serial numbers for asset tracking
- Applying a repeatable troubleshooting process rather than guessing

## How This Applies to My Career

This course was the direct foundation for my work at **Microserve** as a Configuration Technician. I imaged and configured systems at scale for enterprise clients including BC Health Authorities, ICBC, Langara College, Douglas College, KPU, and Westland Insurance. The hardware knowledge and structured troubleshooting process from this course became daily practice — recognizing when a system had a configuration issue versus a hardware fault, and documenting everything accurately for downstream QA and client delivery.

The methodology carried forward to **F12** and **ESW IT**, where hardware troubleshooting on-site for end users requires the same systematic approach.
